B‑Privacy

Defining and Enforcing Privacy in Weighted Voting

Samuel Breckenridge*1, Dani Vilardell*1, Andrés Fábrega1, Amy Zhao1, Patrick McCorry2, Rafael Solari1, Ari Juels1

1Cornell Tech   ·   2Infura   ·   *Equal contribution

Abstract

Weighted-voting systems underpin governance in DAOs and other blockchain-based platforms. Conventional ballot secrecy is not enough in these settings: publishing raw tallies can often reveal how individual voters cast their ballots. We introduce B‑privacy, a framework that captures the transparency–privacy tradeoff through the lens of bribery economics, measuring the economic cost for an adversary to bribe voters to change the outcome, given what a published tally reveals.

We propose a mechanism that adds noise to tallies to enforce B‑privacy, and analyze 3,582 proposals across 30 DAOs. We find that voter concentration fundamentally limits achievable privacy: when outcomes hinge on whales, little can be hidden. Yet for proposals whose outcomes require coalitions of five or more voters, our mechanism delivers a geometric mean 4.1× increase in bribery cost.

Five ballots, two totals. Alice, Bob, Carol, Dave, and Eve cast in secret — but who voted for what?

Whale attack: Alice’s weight of 12 can’t fit inside a total of 11. → In favour.

Whale attack: Only 5 left on the favour side. Bob’s 7 won’t fit. → Against.

Precision attack: The remaining {4, 3, 2} must split into favour = 5, against = 4. Only {4} sums to 4 — Carol. → Against.

Precision attack: That leaves {3, 2} summing to exactly 5 for favour. Dave’s 3. → In favour.

And Eve’s 2 completes the favour side. → In favour. Every ballot exposed.

Noise as a defense

B‑privacy publishes a noised tally instead of the raw count: a small amount of calibrated randomness is added to each option’s total. The outcome stays correct in expectation, but the deduction chain breaks — the attacker can no longer be sure which weight went where.

Each bubble below is one DAO; size encodes the average number of voters per proposal. Bubbles start at the raw tally attack rate and animate to their position under the noised tally.
